During the course of working with our clients,  it has come to light that many, who are large energy users and actively considering implementing Combined Heat and Power (CHP) or Co-generation, but are not fully familiar with  energy contracting , particularly gas, steam electricity  purchasing commercial arrangements and the associated contracts.

 

 

Energy Innovation provide support services to select and monitor gas suppliers and implement commercial arrangements for  energy purchasing.

 

Energy Innovation are keen advocates for gas trading at an Irish Title Transfer Facility sometimes called a virtual balancing point.

 

This is a virtual transfer point where parties have the  opportunity to sell or purchase ( i.e.  transfer title)  gas that is already in gas  system ( i.e. ‘entry-paid gas’).

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

CHP Contracts

 

Energy Innovation have  significant experience in successfully negotiating CHP Build Own Operate and Transfer

( BOOT)  commercial terms and implementing the  contracts.

Our  ability to understand both technical and commercial aspects of the project is key.

For example;

 

“Free Steam” contracts   -  We have advised our clients in relation to commercial  contractual terms with  “free steam”  as part of  a proposed Build Own  Operate and Transfer  (BOOT)  CHP however the BOOT operator  will run the plant to optimise electricity production and not to facilitate the host company’s process

 

Contracts with steam costs based on total energy  not useful energy -  We have advised our clients in relation to commercial  contractual terms where it is proposed that the cost of steam to the client is based on total energy (  total enthalpy Hg) not useful energy ( evaporation enthalpy Hfg ). Many contracts we have seen have proposed these commercial terms and we have successfully negotiated changes on behalf of the client
